<b>Description.</b><br/>A <b>Batch</b> is a specific identified number or quantity of <b>Product</b> or <b>Produce </b>or a mix of Product or Produce.. <br/><br/><b>Remark.</b><br/>A Batch can be packed/stored/transported in one or more Containers. More Containers can belong to the same Batch. Product or Produce can also be stored in a pile on a flat surface.<br/><b><br/></b><b>Remark.</b><br/>When a container is filled with Product or Produce from two different BatchLots, a new Batch is formed.<br/><b><br/></b><b>Remark.</b><br/>A Batch of <b>Produce</b> can originate from; a certain <b>HarvestingZone</b>, another BatchLot, a combination of other Batch'es or a combination from a <b>HarvestingZone</b> with an other BatchLot. <br/>A BatchLot of <b>Product</b> can be applied on a <b>TreatmentZone</b>. Between arrival of the <b>BatchLot</b> of <b>Product</b> from a <b>Supplier</b> and the destination on a <b>TreatmentZone</b>, intermediate <b>BatchLot</b>'s can be formed by splitting and mixing (blending).<br/><br/><b>Remark.</b><br/><b>Allocation</b> is used to describe the transfer from a source (<b>TreatmentZone</b>, <b>Batch</b>, Batch'es) to a destination ( Batch, Batch'es, <b>TreatmentZone</b>).<br/>See <b>Allocation</b> for some examples.<br/>
