<b>Definition.</b><br/>A Field is a continuous surface of land which is bordered by land of an other user, <b>Ditch</b>es, <b>Canal</b>s, roads or a strip of land what is not allowed to be cultivated. <br/><br/><b>Remark.</b><br/>It is the maximum continuous piece of land that can be used to cultivate a crop. The <b>Field</b> will normally stay the same over more years.<br/><br/>When one or more different <b>CropType</b>s are grown on a <b>Field</b>, one or more <b>CropField</b> 's are formed. There might also be a reason for the farmer to split an area with one CropType in different CropFields. ==#gt;A <b>Field</b> exists of one or more<b> CropField</b>'s.<br/><br/>Activities (<b>Task</b>s and <b>Operation</b>s) are executed on an <b>ActivityField</b>, which can correspond with the <b>Field</b> as a whole, but also be a part of the <b>Field</b>. In the latter case an <b>ActivityField</b> will in many cases correspond with a <b>CropField</b>, but it can also combine two or more CropFields, when they require the same type of <b>Operation</b>.<br/><br/>The border with a <b>Ditch</b> or <b>Canal</b> is where the land surface makes an angle with the bank of the Ditch or Canal.<br/>The border with a <b>Road</b> is at the border of the bank along the road.<br/><br/>The border with land of an other user is as is agreed with the other user. Normally this will be the same as the border of a <b>KadastralField</b>, but there are situations where users can agree to deviate from that.<br/>
