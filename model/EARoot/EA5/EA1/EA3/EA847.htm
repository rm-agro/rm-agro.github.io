(Source; Wikipedia.<br/><br/><b>Remark.</b><br/>Recognition of a cultivated <a href="file:///wiki/Plant"><font color="#0000ff"><u>plant</u></font></a> (a <a href="file:///wiki/Cultivar"><font color="#0000ff"><u>cultivar</u></font></a>) as a "variety" in this particular sense provides its <a href="file:///wiki/Plant_breeding"><font color="#0000ff"><u>breeder</u></font></a> with some legal protection, so-called <a href="file:///wiki/Plant_breeders%27_rights"><font color="#0000ff"><u>plant breeders' rights</u></font></a>, depending to some extent on the internal legislation of the UPOV signatory countries, such as the <a href="file:///wiki/Plant_Variety_Protection_Act"><font color="#0000ff"><u>Plant Variety Protection Act</u></font></a> in the US.
